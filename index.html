        Route Tracker User Guide

        1. Getting Started: First-Time Login

        Your first interaction with Route Tracker will be the login screen. Click the "Sign in with Google" button. The application will ask for your permission to access your location; this is essential for the app to function correctly. After logging in, click the Settings icon (⚙️) -> "Edit Profile" to set your display name.

        2. The Interface: Two Roles in One

        Route Tracker has two primary modes: Driver and Dispatcher. You can switch between these modes at any time from the settings menu (⚙️).

        3. Driver Mode: On the Road

        My Current Job: If you are assigned to a job, this panel appears, showing job details. It has buttons to Navigate (opens Google Maps), view Messages, and Complete the job.

        Open Job Postings: Shows available jobs. Click "Accept" to assign a job to yourself.

        Completing a Job: When you click "Complete", a popup will ask for a completion comment. After you submit, the job is sent to dispatch for final review, and you are free to take a new job.

        4. Dispatch Mode: The Command Center

        Creating a Job: Use the "Create Job" button. You can fill in details and either post it as an open job or assign it directly to an available driver.

        Job Dashboard: Manage all jobs here. The "Current" tab shows active jobs, while the "Archive" tab shows closed ones. You can search for jobs using the search bar.

        Finalizing a Job: When a driver completes a job, it will appear with a light green background and a "Finalize" button. Review the driver's comments, then click "Finalize" to add your own closing remarks and officially complete the job.

        Archiving and Deleting: On completed or unassigned jobs, you can "Archive" them to the archive list or "Delete" them permanently.

        5. Administrator Functions

        Users with admin permissions can access the "Admin: User Roles" button in the settings menu. This panel allows you to grant or revoke permissions for all users, including creating jobs, deleting jobs, and managing other users.
